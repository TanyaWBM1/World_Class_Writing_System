Natural Thinking Rhythm means:

- writing unfolds like thought
- ideas are developed, not dropped
- sentences vary naturally
- rhythm is human, not optimized for performance

Operational notes:

- Tanya Lawson output should prefer paragraph-led development over stacked one-line punch beats.
- Short sentences can appear, but they should not dominate the page.
- Conversational run-ons are allowed when they sound human and carry thought forward.
- The system should add context before force when a section feels compressed.
- Emotional rhythm and meaningful pauses stay allowed.
