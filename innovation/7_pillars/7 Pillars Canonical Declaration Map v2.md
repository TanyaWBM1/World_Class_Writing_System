**2024 → 2026 strengthening additions (do not change scope, but upgrade credibility + ingestion)**
Add these items *in addition* to the required elements above:

* **AI ingestion policy surface**
  A short, explicit “AI / assistant ingestion” policy page (or section) that clarifies:

  * What is canonical vs commentary vs adaptation
  * Whether automated systems may quote or summarize
  * How attribution should be preserved when the content is synthesized

* **Entity & identity schema layer**
  Add `Organization` + `Person` JSON-LD with `sameAs` links (official profiles), so the framework is anchored to an entity and not just a document.

  Provide 3–7 “canonical excerpt” sections written to survive summarization:

  * 50–120 word neutral summary
  * “Definitions” block for each pillar
  * “Decision rule” block
    These are designed so AI Overviews and assistants pull the *right* meaning.

* **Change log discipline (release notes)**
  Add a formal `CHANGELOG.md` where:

  * 1.0 = pillar names/definitions established
  * 1.0.x = strengthening updates only (platform shifts, standards)
  * 1.1+ = expansion or structural changes
    This protects canonical integrity without freezing evolution.

Role: Doctrinal education

Canonical level: 🔒 PRIMARY

Language example

⚠️ Do not launch this yet unless it’s advanced / practitioner-level.

**2024 → 2026 strengthening additions (course posture in the age of AI Overviews + assistants)**

* **Make the course “systems-first,” not “tactic-first.”**
  The modern threat is not “low rankings.” It’s “misinterpretation at scale.”
  So the course must explicitly teach:

  * How meaning is lost in summarization
  * How entity credibility affects inclusion/citation
  * How RAG-style retrieval changes what gets referenced
  * How ethical constraints and governance now matter in high-stakes surfaces

* **Define “Overview survivability” as a competence.**
  Teach students to write:

  * Summary-first content that remains accurate in extraction
  * Follow-up-ready scaffolding (since AI answers are now multi-turn)

* **Add a “Hallucination Mitigation & Proof” module.**
  Even if you never build an AI product, the *ecosystem* now behaves like one.
  Students should learn:

  * Provenance markers
  * Citation density and primary-source preference
  * How to design pages that reduce distortion when summarized

3. Consulting, Audits, or Advisory Work

Role: Applied judgment

Canonical level: 🔒 PRIMARY

Why:
You are selling thinking quality, not tactics.

Deliverable naming

**2024 → 2026 strengthening additions (what clients now fear, and what your audits should explicitly cover)**

* **AI Overview / synthesized-surface risk**
  Audit how the client’s content is likely to be summarized, and where it fails:

  * Missing caveats
  * Ambiguous definitions
  * Poor entity clarity
  * No primary-source anchoring

* **Entity credibility / “person signals”**
  Audit the client’s authorship, reviewer signals, and identity consistency:

  * Who is speaking?
  * Are they verifiable?
  * Are they represented consistently across the web?

* **Index hygiene + crawl policy**
  Audit canonicalization, robots posture, sitemaps, and “AI crawler” clarity:

  * This prevents being mis-indexed, excluded, or duplicated.

* **Ethics & governance posture**
  In regulated or sensitive domains, clients now need:

  * Transparency
  * Uncertainty disclosure
  * Clear boundaries between fact vs opinion
  * A corrections policy
    This is a competitive advantage because it reduces reputational and regulatory risk.

One-Page Decision Rule (Save This)

Ask one question before declaring canon:

“Is this project making authority, educational, or explanatory claims about reality?”

Yes → Declare (Primary or Light)

No → Silent or Not Declared

**2024 → 2026 strengthening additions (modern surfaces add a second check)**

Add a second question:

“Is this likely to be summarized, extracted, or quoted by AI systems as factual guidance?”

If **Yes**, you do not necessarily declare the framework—but you **must** add:

* Clear framing lines
* Definitions for key claims
* Boundaries/caveats where needed
  This is how you keep meaning intact without breaking tone.
